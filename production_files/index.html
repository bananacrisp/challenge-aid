ChallengeAid promotes health and fitness throughout the UK by motivating schools, universities, corporate groups and individuals to undertake physical challenges.

We believe that we are one of the very few charities that is able to respond to some of Africa’s on-going issues without creating a culture of dependency or bringing kneejerk reactions to the latest crises.

What are Schools of Hope?

Our “Schools of Hope” operate after school hours, giving children educational support by providing desks, chairs, lighting and most importantly a good supply of up-to-date text books with responsible supervision in a safe and conducive learning environment.

It gives aspirational young people the opportunity to succeed and escape a lifetime of poverty by improving their job prospects and helps to increase self esteem through drama, music and sport at weekends, and enlightens them on life skills and health issues including the negative impact of alcohol, drugs and unprotected sex.

How can you get involved?

If you are a school, a University, a Corporate or an individual hoping to undertake an Open Challenge.

Schoolchildren are encouraged to walk, jog or run a minimum of a mile in the course of an hour as an organised school activity at your school.

Schoolchildren raise £1 each for participating in the programme. The money is collected for ChallengeAid by the school.

The event should be organised on site – on the school running track or playing field, but preferably as part of a sustainable health related fitness programme’
